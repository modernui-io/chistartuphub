# PHASE 1: FOUNDATION

*You're testing whether the problem is real and worth solving.*

## PRODUCT — Problem-Fit

**The Question:** Do people actually have this problem?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] You're talking to potential customers weekly
- [ ] You have a hypothesis but no paying customers yet
- [ ] Your "product" might just be a landing page or prototype

**Warning Signs:**
- Building features no one asked for
- Avoiding customer conversations
- Assuming you know what people want

**Key Metrics:**
- Customer interviews completed: aim for 50+
- Problem validation: Can you describe the pain in their words?
- Willingness to pay: Have 3+ people said "I would pay for this"?

---

## TEAM — Solo/Co-founders

**The Question:** Do you have the right people to start this journey?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] 1-2 founders doing everything
- [ ] Roles are fluid, everyone does everything
- [ ] No formal structure yet

**Warning Signs:**
- Co-founder conflicts brewing (have hard conversations early)
- Skill gaps in product, tech, or business
- No written agreements between founders

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Draft co-founder agreement (equity, roles, vesting)
- [ ] Identify your #1 skill gap
- [ ] Define who makes final decisions in each area

---

## FUNDING — Bootstrapping/Pre-seed

**The Question:** Can you get to the next milestone without running out of runway?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Self-funded or friends & family money
- [ ] <$100K raised total
- [ ] 6-12 months of runway

**Warning Signs:**
- Spending on things that don't help you learn
- No clear milestones for next funding
- Raising too early (before you can answer basic questions)

**Chicago Resources:**
- Illinois SBDC (free business counseling)
- Chicago:Blend pre-accelerator
- SCORE Chicago mentorship

---

## OPERATIONS — Manual/Ad-hoc

**The Question:** Are you capturing what you're learning?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Processes exist in your head, not documented
- [ ] Using free/cheap tools (Notion, Google Sheets)
- [ ] No formal metrics tracking

**Warning Signs:**
- Reinventing the wheel each time
- Can't answer "what did you learn last week?"
- Losing track of customer conversations

**Starter Stack:**
- Communication: Slack (free tier)
- Docs: Notion or Google Docs
- CRM: Spreadsheet or HubSpot (free)
- Analytics: Basic Google Analytics

# PHASE 2: TRACTION

*You've found something that works. Now make it repeatable.*

## PRODUCT — Product-Market Fit

**The Question:** Do people love this enough to pay and tell others?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Revenue is growing (even if small)
- [ ] Users are sticking around (retention > 40%)
- [ ] Word-of-mouth is happening

**Warning Signs:**
- Users sign up but don't come back
- Sales require heavy discounting
- Every customer wants something different

**PMF Indicators:**
- NPS > 40
- Users angry when product is down
- Organic growth happening

---

## TEAM — Core Team (3-10)

**The Question:** Can you build a team that executes without you in every meeting?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] First 3-5 employees hired
- [ ] Some specialization emerging
- [ ] Starting to think about culture

**Warning Signs:**
- Founder doing everything still
- First hires aren't working out
- No one can make decisions without you

**Action Items:**
- [ ] Document your values (actually live them)
- [ ] Create onboarding process
- [ ] Delegate one major area completely

---

## FUNDING — Seed/Angel

**The Question:** Can you raise enough to find repeatable growth?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Raising $500K-$2M
- [ ] Have some traction to show
- [ ] Pitching to angels and seed VCs

**Warning Signs:**
- Raising on story alone (need some metrics)
- Taking bad terms out of desperation
- Not tracking where every dollar goes

**Chicago Investors (Seed Stage):**
- Chicago Ventures
- M25
- Hyde Park Venture Partners
- LOUD Capital
- Lightbank

---

## OPERATIONS — Process Building

**The Question:** Can someone else do this task without you explaining it?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Some processes documented
- [ ] Starting to use real tools (CRM, project management)
- [ ] Basic metrics dashboards exist

**Warning Signs:**
- Knowledge lives only in people's heads
- New hires take months to become productive
- Same mistakes happening repeatedly

**Process Priorities:**
1. Sales process (lead → close)
2. Customer support workflow
3. Product development cycle
4. Hiring process

---

# PHASE 3: SCALE

*You know what works. Now do it 10x bigger.*

## PRODUCT — Market Expansion

**The Question:** Can this work in new segments or markets?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Core product stable
- [ ] Exploring adjacent markets
- [ ] Building platform/ecosystem plays

**Warning Signs:**
- Expanding before core is solid
- Losing focus on what made you successful
- Technical debt slowing everything down

---

## TEAM — Organization Building

**The Question:** Can you build a company that outlasts any individual?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] 20+ employees
- [ ] Multiple layers of management
- [ ] Formal HR and operations

**Warning Signs:**
- Culture diluting with growth
- Communication breaking down
- A-players leaving

---

## FUNDING — Series A+

**The Question:** Can you deploy large amounts of capital efficiently?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Raising $5M+
- [ ] Clear path to $10M+ ARR
- [ ] Institutional investors leading

**Chicago Growth Investors:**
- Pritzker Group
- OCA Ventures
- Valor Equity Partners
- Jump Capital

---

## OPERATIONS — Automated Systems

**The Question:** Do your systems scale without linear headcount growth?

**You're Here If:**
- [ ] Significant automation in place
- [ ] Data-driven decision making
- [ ] Predictable, repeatable processes

# GLOSSARY

**ARR** — Annual Recurring Revenue

**Burn Rate** — Monthly cash spent

**CAC** — Customer Acquisition Cost (cost to get one customer)

**Churn** — Rate at which customers leave

**ICP** — Ideal Customer Profile

**LTV** — Lifetime Value (total revenue from one customer)

**MRR** — Monthly Recurring Revenue

**NPS** — Net Promoter Score (would customers recommend you?)

**PMF** — Product-Market Fit

**Runway** — Months of cash remaining

**TAM/SAM/SOM** — Total/Serviceable/Obtainable Market

**Unit Economics** — Profit per customer after all costs

# FINAL THOUGHTS

Building a startup is hard. You'll feel lost most days. That's normal.

The founders who succeed aren't smarter—they're better at:
1. **Learning fast** — Talk to customers obsessively
2. **Admitting mistakes** — Kill ideas that aren't working
3. **Building support** — Find mentors, co-founders, community

**This atlas is a compass, not a map.** Your journey will look different. The phases won't be linear. You'll skip ahead in some areas and fall behind in others.

That's the game. Keep playing.
